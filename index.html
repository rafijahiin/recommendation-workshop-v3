(i) Purpose & Objectives of the Evaluation    

 
   
       
     

Purpose

     

        Provide accountability for results and use of resources, support         evidence-based decision-making across the humanitarian–development–peace nexus,         and drive organizational learning to improve how UNFPA Bangladesh achieves the         Strategic Plan transformative results. Insights will inform the next CPD (with CP10 extended through Dec 2026).      

   
       
     

Objectives

     
           
  • Assess relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, coherence, sustainability, and humanitarian connectedness—especially for those left behind.
    •        
  • Generate practical, prioritized conclusions and recommendations to guide 2025–2026 execution and the next CPD.
    •        
  • Strengthen the use of evidence (ToCs, LNOB data, PD insights) in programming, budgeting, and partnership choices.
    •

      (iii) Evaluation Methods    

   

How the evaluation was conducted

 
   
       
     
Approach
     
       

Theory-based Evaluation

       

          Anchored in four thematic Theories of Change (ToCs), using contribution analysis to test assumptions and system linkages.        

     
   
       
     
Design
     
       

Mixed-methods

       

          Triangulation of evidence through document review, key informant interviews (KIIs), focus group discussions (FGDs), observations, and secondary quantitative data.        

     
   
       
     
Inclusion
     
       

Participatory & Rights-based

       

          Designed with a Leave No One Behind lens — ensuring voices of vulnerable groups (PWD, adolescents, Rohingya communities) are included.        

     
   
       
     
Ethics
     
       

Ethical Safeguards

       

          Informed consent, privacy, and protection of vulnerable groups applied consistently across data collection and analysis.        

     
   
 
   
    UNFPA Teal → core design; Orange → mixed-methods; Gold → LNOB; Navy → ethics.

      (v) Summary of the Conclusions    

   

Concise, actionable takeaways that drive the recommendations

 
   
   
           
                 
            C1            

A single, risk-informed Umbrella ToC is needed

         
                         
          Current work streams follow separate logics; a unified ToC would align AWPs/budgets, surface cross-theme risks/assumptions, and enable joint course-correction.        
     
           
                 
            C2            

Proven models exist but are not yet standardized for scale

         
                         
          SOPs, QA/fidelity tools, and budget codes are uneven. Without a standard package per model, adoption by ministries beyond pilots remains slow.        
     
           
                 
            C3            

Equity performance is not yet “managed to” (LNOB visibility gaps)

         
                         
          Data are disaggregated but targets/floors (e.g., referral closure, accessibility compliance) are not consistently set or linked to disbursement calendars.        
     
           
                 
            C4            

Execution bottlenecks reduce timeliness, quality, and VfM

         
                         
          Stop–start delivery, long procurement lead times, and stockouts drive emergency buys and uneven last-mile performance, hurting LNOB coverage.        
     
           
                 
            C5            

Durable results require moving from funding to financing

         
                         
          Without MTEF-coded lines and routine budget execution, continuity (esp. LNOB) remains vulnerable to short-cycle grants.        
     
           
                 
            C6            

Innovation is present, but not yet strategic

         
                         
          Pilots are scattered; a protocol with stage-gates, inclusion/safety checks, and budget/procurement enablement is needed to move from “pilot” to “standard.”

R1

Adopt a unified, risk-informed Umbrella ToC

High

Rationale

One shared ToC aligns AWPs/budgets, makes risks/assumptions explicit, and enables joint course-correction and adaptive management.

Operational Implications

  • Co-design sprint (4–6 weeks) to map outcomes, indicators, assumptions/risks.
  • Map all outputs/AWPs/budget lines to Umbrella ToC; monthly joint reviews; 10–12 core indicators dashboard.
Provide feedback
R2

Standardize and scale-up proven models

High

Rationale

Standard packages (SOPs, QA/fidelity, indicators) + budget codes enable adoption by ministries beyond pilots and across districts.

Operational Implications

  • One specification per model; replication kits (policy brief, cost note, SOPs).
  • Quarterly harvest of good practices; adoption–quality–equity dashboard.
Provide feedback
R3

Make equity visibility & performance non-negotiable (LNOB)

High

Rationale

Disaggregation isn’t enough; set clear targets/floors (parity, referral closure, accessibility) and manage to them.

Operational Implications

  • Define a lean LNOB indicator pack; set national + district floors in AWPs/MoUs.
  • Link equity floors to disbursement calendars; WG-SS safeguards and data quality training.
Provide feedback
R4

Fix execution bottlenecks for continuity and efficiency

High

Rationale

Stop–start delivery inflates costs and harms LNOB outreach; predictable disbursement/procurement and reliable stocks are core.

Operational Implications

  • Lock monthly cash release & procurement calendars; procurement SLA (e.g., ≤45 days competitive).
  • ≥95% stock reliability for tracer commodities; referral transport slots; Q4 spend cap (≤30%).
Provide feedback
R5

Shift from funding to financing with a VfM/ROI compact

High

Rationale

Appropriations (MTEF-coded, routine budgets) stabilize service continuity and protect LNOB lines.

Operational Implications

  • Agree roadmap with MoF/ERD; cost policy wins; assign MTEF/budget codes and AWP lines (incl. resilience/LNOB).
  • Pooled procurement/framework agreements; light VfM/ROI pack in AWP reviews; bridge financing calendars.
Provide feedback
R6

Move from innovations to strategic innovation

Medium-High

Rationale

Focus scarce resources on ToC-critical problems; validate for norms, indicators, and budgets; enable SSTC portability.

Operational Implications

  • Approve stage-gates; small Strategic Innovation Review Board; 1-page evidence/cost notes.
  • Budget/procurement enablement; equity & safety gates; scale dashboard; time-boxed IP pilots.
Provide feedback

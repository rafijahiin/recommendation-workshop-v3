(i) Purpose & Objectives of the Evaluation

Purpose

Provide accountability for results and use of resources; support evidence-based decision-making across the humanitarian–development–peace nexus; and drive organizational learning to improve how UNFPA Bangladesh achieves Strategic Plan results. Insights inform the next CPD (with CP10 extended through Dec 2026).

Objectives

  • Assess relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, coherence, sustainability, and humanitarian connectedness—especially for those left behind.
  • Generate prioritized conclusions and recommendations to guide 2025–2026 execution and the next CPD.
  • Strengthen the use of evidence (ToCs, LNOB data, PD insights) in programming, budgeting, and partnerships.

(ii) Evaluation Questions

Aligned to OECD-DAC criteria + humanitarian connectedness

Relevance
1

Are interventions relevant and addressing those left behind?

Alignment to national priorities, SDGs/ICPD, and needs of marginalized populations (LNOB, disability).

Adaptiveness
2

Do the ToCs help adapt to a dynamic context and megatrends?

Fitness of thematic ToCs to guide adaptation in programming and risk management.

Coherence
3

How coherent is the portfolio internally and with partners?

Internal/external coherence; consistent LNOB and disability inclusion.

Partnerships & Innovation
4

Do partnerships/innovation strengthen access and norms?

Contribution to normative role, access/quality, systems change, incl. SSTC.

Effectiveness
5

What results were achieved, especially for LNOB groups?

Progress against outcomes, systems contributions, and unintended effects.

Efficiency
6

How efficiently were resources used to reach those left behind?

Timeliness, continuity (cash/procurement/logistics), value for money.

Sustainability
7

Are results likely to last?

Capacities, norms/policies, financing (MTEF), ownership at all levels.

Humanitarian Connectedness
8

Is the humanitarian response connected to longer-term outcomes?

Contribution of the Rohingya response to resilience and transitions.

(iii) Evaluation Methods

How the evaluation was conducted

Approach

Theory-based Evaluation

Anchored in four thematic ToCs, using contribution analysis to test assumptions and system linkages.

Design

Mixed-methods

Triangulation via document review, KIIs, FGDs, observations, and secondary quantitative data.

Inclusion

Participatory & Rights-based

Designed with a strong LNOB lens—including voices of PWD, adolescents, and Rohingya communities.

Ethics

Ethical Safeguards

Informed consent, privacy, and protection of vulnerable groups throughout data collection and analysis.

    Coherence improving; standards uneven across partners

    Coherence Partnerships
    • Good external coordination; internal standards (LNOB fields, QA/fidelity) not uniform in all IPs.

    Effectiveness Systems

    Stop–start execution increases costs and undermines last-mile

    Efficiency Logistics
    • Long procurement lead times and Q4 compression drive emergency buys and idle capacity.

    (v) Summary of the Conclusions

    Concise, actionable takeaways that drive the recommendations

    C1

    A single, risk-informed Umbrella ToC is needed
    Separate logics persist; a unified ToC aligns AWPs/budgets, surfaces cross-theme risks, and enables joint course-correction.
    C2

    Proven models exist but are not standardized for scale
    SOPs/QA/budget coding uneven; without standard packages, ministry adoption beyond pilots is slow.
    C3

    Equity performance isn’t yet “managed to”
    Data disaggregated, but floors/targets (e.g., referral closure, accessibility) not consistently set or linked to disbursements.
    C4

    Execution bottlenecks hurt timeliness, quality, VfM
    Stop–start delivery, long lead times, stockouts → emergency buys and uneven last mile, especially for LNOB outreach.
    C5

    Durable results need financing (not ad-hoc funding)
    MTEF-coded lines and routine budget execution stabilize continuity, including LNOB lines.
    C6

    Innovation exists, but not yet strategic
    Need protocol with stage-gates, inclusion/safety checks, and budget/procurement enablement to move from pilot → standard.

    (vi) Recommendations

    Click a card to see Rationale + Operational Implications. Filter by priority.

    R1

    Adopt a unified, risk-informed Umbrella ToC

    High

    Rationale

    One shared ToC aligns AWPs/budgets, makes risks/assumptions explicit, and enables joint course-correction and adaptive management.

    Operational Implications

    • Co-design sprint (4–6 weeks) to map outcomes, indicators, assumptions/risks.
    • Map all outputs/AWPs/budget lines to Umbrella ToC; monthly joint reviews; 10–12 core indicators dashboard.
    Provide feedback
    R2

    Standardize and scale-up proven models

    High

    Rationale

    Standard packages (SOPs, QA/fidelity, indicators) + budget codes enable adoption by ministries beyond pilots.

    Operational Implications

    • One specification per model; replication kits (policy brief, cost note, SOPs).
    • Quarterly harvest of good practices; adoption–quality–equity dashboard.
    Provide feedback
    R3

    Make equity visibility & performance non-negotiable (LNOB)

    High

    Rationale

    Disaggregation isn’t enough; set clear targets/floors (parity, referral closure, accessibility) and manage to them.

    Operational Implications

    • Define a lean LNOB indicator pack; set national + district floors in AWPs/MoUs.
    • Link equity floors to disbursement calendars; WG-SS safeguards and data quality training.
    Provide feedback
    R4

    Fix execution bottlenecks for continuity and efficiency

    High

    Rationale

    Stop–start delivery inflates costs and harms LNOB outreach; predictability and stock reliability are core.

    Operational Implications

    • Lock monthly cash release & procurement calendars; procurement SLA ≤45 days (competitive).
    • ≥95% stock reliability; referral transport slots; Q4 spend cap ≤30%.
    Provide feedback
    R5

    Shift from funding to financing with a VfM/ROI compact

    High

    Rationale

    Appropriations (MTEF-coded, routine budgets) stabilize service continuity and protect LNOB lines.

    Operational Implications

    • Roadmap with MoF/ERD; cost policy wins; assign MTEF/budget codes and AWP lines (incl. resilience/LNOB).
    • Pooled procurement/framework agreements; light VfM/ROI pack; bridge financing calendars.
    Provide feedback
    R6

    Move from innovations to strategic innovation

    Medium-High

    Rationale

    Focus scarce resources on ToC-critical problems; validate for norms, indicators, and budgets; enable SSTC portability.

    Operational Implications

    • Approve stage-gates; small Strategic Innovation Review Board; 1-page evidence/cost notes.
    • Budget/procurement enablement; equity & safety gates; scale dashboard; time-boxed IP pilots.
    Provide feedback
